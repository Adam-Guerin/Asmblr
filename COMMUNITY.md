## 🎯 Community Guidelines

### Our Values
- **🤝 Inclusivity**: Welcome everyone regardless of experience level
- **🧩 Collaboration**: Work together to solve problems
- **📚 Learning**: Share knowledge and help others grow
- **🚀 Innovation**: Encourage creative and experimental approaches
- **🎯 Quality**: Maintain high standards in contributions and interactions

### Code of Conduct

#### Be Respectful and Inclusive
- Welcome newcomers and help them learn
- Use inclusive language and avoid assumptions
- Respect different perspectives and experiences
- Focus on constructive feedback and improvement

#### Be Helpful and Supportive
- Answer questions when you can
- Share your knowledge and experiences
- Point people to relevant resources
- Celebrate others' successes

#### Be Professional and Constructive
- Keep discussions focused and productive
- Provide specific, actionable feedback
- Acknowledge good work and contributions
- Report issues respectfully with clear details

#### Be Ethical and Responsible
- Respect privacy and confidentiality
- Don't share proprietary information
- Follow licensing and attribution requirements
- Report security vulnerabilities responsibly

### Communication Guidelines

#### GitHub Issues
- **Search first**: Check existing issues before creating new ones
- **Be specific**: Provide clear, detailed descriptions
- **Use templates**: Follow the provided issue templates
- **One issue per topic**: Keep discussions focused

#### GitHub Discussions
- **Choose the right category**: Use appropriate labels
- **Be patient**: Community members volunteer their time
- **Stay on topic**: Keep discussions relevant
- **Mark solutions**: Help others find answers quickly

#### Pull Requests
- **Describe changes**: Explain what and why you changed something
- **Link issues**: Reference related issues or discussions
- **Test thoroughly**: Ensure your changes work as expected
- **Be responsive**: Address feedback in a timely manner

## 🌍 Community Structure

### Roles and Responsibilities

#### Core Team
- **Maintainers**: Review code, manage releases, set direction
- **Moderators**: Facilitate discussions, enforce guidelines
- **Contributors**: Active community members with regular contributions

#### Community Members
- **Users**: People using Asmblr for their projects
- **Helpers**: Members who assist others in discussions
- **Advocates**: Members who promote and represent Asmblr

#### Becoming a Contributor
1. **Start Small**: Fix typos, improve documentation, answer questions
2. **Build Consistency**: Regular, quality contributions over time
3. **Demonstrate Expertise**: Show deep understanding of the codebase
4. **Community Trust**: Earn respect through helpful, reliable participation
